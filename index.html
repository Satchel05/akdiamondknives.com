I’m a young, amature knifemaker who has over a year of experience, and over 20 knives made. Making knives is one of my favorite things to do and I am thankful that I have the time and resources to do it. I am self taught, and I am still learning how to improve and expand into new techniques. Making knives is not my job, but it’s a hobby I am very passionate about. Because of that, I don’t really charge anything for the time I spend. When I make knives I try to stay focused on all of the details so that I can produce something both functional and beautiful. I strongly value customer satisfaction; it feels uncomfortable on a personal level for me to know that a customer isn’t 100% satisfied with their knife. I am happy to give refunds and/or receive your feedback.

Bushcraft, hunting, general outdoors knives: This is the type of knife I have most experience with. I can make a variety of shapes and sizes of this type of knife. I also make edc knives, that are generally for lighter use than the above knives. Some people have a specific shape they want, which I also make. Add pic 14 as well. I recently made my first chef knife and would love to make more. I can also make smaller paring knives. I can’t make knives that have blades more than 8.5 inches long. Also I can’t make knives that are wider than 2 inches and knives that are wider than 1.5 inches will cost more. I only work with flat stock, so I can’t make icepick/tri-dagger style knives. Also, I can’t make blades with a concave curve in the blade (for example a karambit). Lastly, all of my knives are full tang. Blade Finishes Currently I only do hand sanded finishes and vinegar etching. A mirror finish will cost a little more and take longer. If you want acid and stone washing please let me know because I am considering buying the necessary equipment. I currently don’t have it so it may take longer if you are the first customer to ask for it. The picture below is a knife with a vinegar etch, the rest of my knives on this site are not etched. I have a neighbor who makes custom molded kydex sheaths. They cost 15$, but possibly more for special things like belt clips. I can also have him make sheaths for knives that you may own, but they would have to be shipped to me and back which would add to the price.

I make my knives to order. If you want a knife, email me at akdiamondknives@gmail.com. This website is just for information, I handle most things through my email. You can email me a picture of a knife you want made, or just describe what you want. Next, I will send you a sketch of the knife, and we can tweak it so that you get exactly what you want. I will also ask about what type of blade finish, handle material, etc you want. I take my payment through Paypal after I have finished and will notify you first if there are any small issues with the knife. In the past some of my knives have had very slight warping or a few scratches, and I prefer to mention it before shipping things off. I also want to mention that as far as I know my customers were all satisfied with their knives and most sent back emails saying that they really liked them. I mostly make knives one at a time and I keep a list of people in the order that they asked for a knife. If you change your mind about anting a knife that's fine just let me know.

My base price is $50 with shipping included. If you want a longer knife, a more time consuming finish, or a knife made with materials I don’t have it may cost more. Other things may also affect the price. I will tell you the price before I start on the knife. If you are not satisfied, I will offer a refund for all but $10 (provided you send the knife back). If the knife breaks and you think it’s my fault, let me know and I may fix it or give you a refund. This isn’t an official warranty, but I would feel bad about selling a knife that doesn’t work properly; and I want to make things right.

I can also restore and fix broken knives. Even if they are not broken I can put a new handle on a knife that has a full tang. If they are expensive or irreplaceable I don’t want to work on them because there is a possibility I could damage or break them. Below is a picture of a sword that I restored.
